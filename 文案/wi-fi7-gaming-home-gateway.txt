Wi-Fi 7 Gaming
Home Gateway
Shaping Performance into Product Identity.
As Gemtek's first flagship model for the Wi-Fi 7 generation and a key product for overseas launch, this project needed to communicate more than a technical upgrade. It had to make next-generation performance visible through a distinctive and memorable product presence.
With its high-performance positioning, my design strategy focused on gaming users who value speed, control, and immersive experiences. Instead of following the common gaming-product language of exaggerated mechanical aggression, I aimed to create a more refined and premium performance expression. The design draws inspiration from the dynamic tension of sports cars and the streamlined power of the orca, using a forward-leaning stance, sculpted side surfaces, vertical lighting, and controlled mechanical details to create a product that feels powerful, emotional, and distinctive without becoming overly aggressive.
This design direction later developed into a full 2023 product program. I was responsible for carrying the concept through ID development, CMF definition, 3D refinement, prototyping, and pre-mass-production confirmation, ensuring the final product could translate a bold design vision into a feasible flagship gateway with strong market presence.

Cooling Design
Thermal Performance, Designed Into Form
By integrating thermal engineering with industrial design, the product transforms airflow requirements into visible design features. Vent openings, internal heat paths, and sculpted surface details work together to improve cooling efficiency while reinforcing the product's technical identity.
CAE airflow and thermal mapping were used to validate the design direction, ensuring stable performance during long gaming sessions and heavy network loads.

Mockup Stage
CMF and Cosmetic Quality Control
During the mockup stage, I reviewed and refined ID/CMF parts across plastic and metal components, focusing on color accuracy, texture consistency, gloss control, label application, assembly gaps, and mechanical feasibility. Working closely with the ME team, I helped resolve tooling limitations, wall-thickness risks, parting-line issues, and structural details to align design intent with production quality.
